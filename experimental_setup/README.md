# Experimental Setup

The sand used in these experiments had a measured grain size distribution, with characteristic particle diameters of  D10 = 0.15 mm, D50 = 0.50 mm, and D90 = 1.0 mm. The sand samples were prepared by mixing dry sand with water to achieve a target moisture content of 5%. For each batch, the total sand mass was first measured, and the bucket weight was subtracted to obtain the net dry sand mass. The required water volume was then calculated as:
V_w=0.05×M_s
where V_wis the required water volume in liters, and M_sis the dry sand mass in kilograms. This calculation assumes the density of water is approximately 1 kg/L.

The calculated amount of water was added to the dry sand and mixed using an electric cement mixer to promote uniform moisture distribution throughout the batch. A batch preparation log was maintained during the experiments to document the measured sand weights, corrected dry sand masses, and corresponding water volumes required to reach the target 5% moisture content.
After mixing, the moist sand was placed into the experimental flume in 15-cm-thick layers. Each layer was compacted using 10 blows from a 7.5-kg hand tamper dropped from a height of 15 cm. This layer-by-layer placement and compaction procedure was adopted to improve sample uniformity and to achieve a consistent dry density throughout the flume.
